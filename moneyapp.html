MoneyApp: UX Case Study

Overview

MoneyApp, is a payment application that helps with faster online payments, secure money transfers and a platform to invest in stocks/cryptocurrency. Its main purpose is to provide multiple app features in a single platform without compromising on its security and trustworthiness. Its easy-to-use navigate characteristic and well-designed user-interface makes it worth your time & money.

Design Thinking Approach

Problem Statement

In today's generation where everythingis online and quick, people need an app that will handle their money and help with safe transactions. A secure and trustworthy app along with an easy-to-use design is needed wherein users will feel safe while inputing their personal card details, making them less likely to get caught up in scams.

We will know this to be true when different users can easily navigate through the app and successfully perform money transactions without being caught up in scams/frauds.

Solution

  • Make a secure & trust-worthy app that safely handles user's money without compromising their personal details.
  • Have an easy-to-navigate interface to ease user experience.

Research Goals

Participants

Affinity Maps

After conducting my user research, I used Affinity Maps as a method to organize & synthesize the data I had gathered so far. This allowed me to identify patterns, themes , and trends in data which helped me gain insights into users' needs and preferences.

Insights

  • Most users want an app to remember their card details for them
  • Security & Convenience are the top priority for all users
  • While many users have switched to online methods of payment, people still prefer swiping a card or paying by cash
  • Many users want the app to be integrated with more vendors
  • All users prefer a simple and easy-to-use interface.
  • Users prefer familiarity over change as every process has the “unknown” factor associated with it.
  • Users are looking for apps that include online payment, money transfer and stock trading all-in-one so they don't have to install several apps.
  • Speed, security and easy-to-navigate features are essential as this app deals with money.

User Personas

Based on my research and data I created 2 User Personas that represent my target users. They will help me understand my users' needs, behaviors, and motivations that will help me create more effective solutions. In my case, both my personas are from different generations and can be categorized into Gen Z and Gen X. I also made User Journey Maps for each to improve user experience for different generations

Usability Testing

6 participants

12-15 questions

23-27 May 2023

I conducted a Usability Test with 6 participants to test my prototype. As I observed different participants interacting with my prototype, it helped me identify various usability issues and got me feedback on how to improve the user experience. I also identified minor pain-points for users which had I not done this usability test I would have never discovered it.

Usability Test Report

The Usability Report consisted of numerous changes as well as usability issues. Listed below are 5 main issues that most users had faced during their usability test, alongside the implemented changes to the prototype.

Implemented Changes based on Report

Issue 1

Severity: HIGH

  • Deals section needs to be smaller or moved elsewhere from the homepage
  • Need to add an easy access option for "Send/Request" money
  • Add an option by which users can customize the homepage according to their needs and usage

Evidence: All participants had different issues regarding the layout and content of the homepage

Issue 2

Severity: HIGH

  • The entire contact row needs to be clickable
  • The "Send/Request" icon is bolder than other icons and needs to be fixed
  • Should have back-arrow buttons on the top-left corner

Evidence: All participants struggled with some or the other options while navigating the app

Issue 3

Severity: HIGH

  • The boldness and sizes of the navigation bar icons all need to be the same
  • The "Send/Request" icon is bolder than the other icons which needs to be fixed
  • The icon should be highlighted based on what page the user is in
  • The navigation bar needs to be displayed on all pages

Evidence: Three out of six people faced issues with the navigation bar

Issue 4

Severity: MEDIUM

  • Shouldn't have more than 2-3 colors per page
  • The color combination needs to be subtle and not too bright for the eyes

Evidence: Three out of six people found colors distractful/bright.

Issue 5

Severity: MEDIUM

  • Need to address payment method using wallet page in the onboarding section
  • Need to display the amount of money the user has in all accounts.

Evidence: Three out of six people disn't choose wallet page to make online payments.

Retrospective

This project happens to be my first UI/UX project and it feels amazing to look back and see how far I have come.

I think that the most challenging part for me would be gathering different elements and putting all my work together, but it feels rewarding to see the final product.

The most fun part about this whole process was designing the UI for my project from scratch. It was really fun making various prototypes, choosing the color scheme and placing different elements on the screen. It made me learn a lot of things. Also conducting different interviews and tests helped me gain a different perspective which me as a designer wouldn't have seen it. My interviewees gave different suggestions based on their experiences from different apps which helped me develop my app more effectively.

Future Improvements

  • I believe, in the future users might ask for an AI feature in the app which can predict what the users want and make their experience with the app easier an help reach their goals. I plan on using artificial intelligence to solve user problems related to this app such as fraud detection, help with secure payments, etc. I feel that the future of AI will be exciting, with advancements in natural language processing, computer vision and machine learning. However, it's also important to ensure that AI is used ethically and responsibly, especially where money is involved.
  • I also plan on adding more screens and flows to the app to make it fully functional and ready to launch.
  • I would also conduct more usability tests in the future to test new prototypes and features and get more feedback and app suggestions for the same.
